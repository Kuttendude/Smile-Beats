Background Research & Problem Definition

Understanding the challenge and who it affects before designing a solution.

← Back to Home

The Problem

Kids often resist or rush through brushing their teeth. Dentists recommend brushing for two full minutes, twice a day, but studies show most children brush for 30 seconds or less. This leads to plaque buildup, cavities, and poor oral health habits that can last a lifetime. Parents struggle to enforce brushing routines without turning it into a daily battle.

Who It Affects

Primary audiences include children ages 3–10 and their parents or caregivers. Pediatric dentists and educators who promote oral health are also key stakeholders. The problem is widespread — nearly every family with young children faces some form of brushing resistance.

Research Summary

  • Placeholder: Study on average brushing duration among children.
  • Placeholder: Parent survey on brushing challenges and motivation techniques.
  • Placeholder: Dentist recommendations for effective brushing routines.
  • Placeholder: Role of music and gamification in child behavior change.

Key Insights

Time matters

Kids who brush for 2 minutes have significantly better oral health outcomes than those who rush.

Engagement is key

Activities that make brushing fun increase compliance. Music and play are powerful motivators.

Parents need support

Parents want tools that help kids brush independently without constant nagging.

Problem Statement

Young children do not brush their teeth for the recommended two minutes, leading to preventable oral health issues. Parents lack effective, non-confrontational tools to encourage proper brushing habits. We need a solution that makes brushing engaging, timed, and parent-friendly.
