Key Design Goals (Solution Requirements)

Before choosing our final design, we ranked the most important requirements for our toothbrush. Our top priorities were affordability, brushing tracking, parent notifications, a 2-minute music feature, comfort for children, and long-term use.

Affordable

Needed to cost less than $45 while still working well.

Tracks Brushing

Needed to tell whether brushing actually happened.

Parent Notifications

Parents should know if their child brushed or not.

2-Minute Music

Needed to keep children engaged for the full brushing time.

Comfortable for Kids

Needed to fit inside a child’s mouth and feel comfortable.

Long-Term Use

Needed to last over time and allow replacement tops.

Competitors / Existing Solutions

We researched products already on the market to see what features existed and where they fell short. Many products helped with timing or entertainment, but not all of them gave parents enough accountability.

Colgate hum Kids

This toothbrush uses app-based interaction and lets parents track brushing.

Disadvantage: It is expensive and replacement tops can be hard to find.

Philips Sonicare for Kids

This toothbrush supports a two-minute routine and includes an app experience.

Disadvantage: Kids can still fake brushing, and parent monitoring is limited.

7AM2M Electric Toothbrush

This toothbrush has long battery life and 30-second reminders.

Disadvantage: It is not especially fun for children and lacks strong monitoring.

Potential Solutions

Music Timer Toothbrush

Fun, but does not verify brushing.

App-Based Tracker

Easy to build, but relies on honesty.

Parent Monitoring Device

Strong accountability, but not very engaging.

Brush Buddy (Final Idea)

Combines fun, tracking, and parent notifications.

Chosen Design

We chose Brush Buddy because it combines entertainment, tracking, and accountability. Unlike other solutions, it does not just time brushing — it helps confirm that brushing actually happens.

Competitive Advantage (why design is innovative)

Brush Buddy stands out because it does more than just encourage brushing — it verifies it. Most competitors rely on timers or apps, but our design adds tracking and parent notifications, making it more reliable and useful.

Design Changes Made Based on Feedback

Our design remained consistent throughout the ideation process. Feedback confirmed that our original idea was already the strongest because it combined fun, tracking, and accountability in one system.
