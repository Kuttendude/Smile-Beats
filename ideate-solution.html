Ideate Solution

Exploring concepts and selecting the right approach for Brush Buddy.

← Back to Home

Key Design Goals (Solution Requirements)

Before choosing our final design, we ranked our requirements using a numeric system: High = 3, Medium = 2, Low = 1, and Not at all = 0. This helped us focus on the features that would matter most for both children and parents.

Affordable

Our toothbrush needed to cost less than $45 while still being worth buying and maintaining strong quality.

Tracks Brushing

It needed to track whether the child actually brushed so parents could know what happened.

Parent Notifications

Parents should be told if their child did not brush, making the product more useful and accountable.

2-Minute Music Feature

The toothbrush needed to make music for two minutes to keep children entertained for the full brushing time.

Comfort + Child Fit

The toothbrush needed to be comfortable and small enough to fit properly inside a child’s mouth.

Long-Term Use

It needed to work over time and allow replacement tops so families would continue using it.

Our most important requirements were affordability, brushing tracking, parent notification, and keeping children engaged for the full two minutes.

Competitors / Existing Solutions

We researched products already on the market to see what features existed and where they fell short. While many products help with timing or entertainment, not all of them give parents enough accountability.

Colgate hum Kids Manual Toothbrushes

This toothbrush uses soft bristles and an app-based system to make brushing more fun. It also allows parents to track how their children brush and how long they brush for.

Disadvantage: It is expensive, tracking may become less useful over time, and replacement tops can be hard to find.

Philips Sonicare for Kids

This toothbrush supports a two-minute brushing routine, has soft bristles, and includes an app experience to make brushing more interactive.

Disadvantage: Kids can still fake brushing, and parents cannot clearly monitor the child’s full brushing process.

7AM2M Electric Toothbrush

This toothbrush has a long battery life, multiple heads for different users, and 30-second reminders to change where you are brushing.

Disadvantage: It is not especially fun for children and does not provide strong parent monitoring.

Potential Solutions

During brainstorming, we explored several possible ways to solve the problem of children rushing or skipping brushing. Some ideas focused more on fun, while others focused more on tracking or parent communication.

Music Timer Toothbrush

A toothbrush that plays music for two minutes to keep children engaged while brushing.

App-Based Tracker

An app that uses music, reminders, and a timer to make brushing more interactive and fun.

Parent Monitoring Device

A toothbrush designed to send updates to parents so they know whether brushing happened or not.

Brush Buddy (Final Idea)

A toothbrush that combines music, brushing tracking, and parent notification in one system.

Chosen Design

We chose Brush Buddy because it best matched our top design goals. It combines music, brushing tracking, and parent accountability in one design. Unlike other solutions, it does not just time brushing — it helps confirm that brushing actually happens.

Decision Matrix (used to select design)

We compared all of our possible solutions using a decision matrix based on our key requirements. This helped us choose the design that best balanced impact, feasibility, and usefulness.

Competitive Advantage (why design is innovative)

Brush Buddy stands out because it does more than just encourage brushing — it verifies it. Most competitors rely on timers or apps, but our design adds brushing tracking and parent notifications, making it more reliable and useful.

It also balances fun and function. The music feature keeps children engaged for the full two minutes, while the tracking and notification features help parents know whether brushing actually happened.

Design Changes Made Based on Feedback

Our design remained consistent throughout the ideation process. After comparing competitors, reviewing our requirements, and evaluating possible solutions, we found that our original concept already best addressed the problem we were trying to solve.

Because of this, we did not make major design changes based on feedback. Instead, the feedback confirmed that Brush Buddy was the strongest option because it combined fun, tracking, and accountability in one system.
