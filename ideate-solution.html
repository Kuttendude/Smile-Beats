Key Design Goals (Solution Requirements)

Before choosing our final design, we ranked the most important requirements using a numeric system (High = 3, Medium = 2, Low = 1). This helped us focus on what actually mattered.

Affordable

Needed to be under $45 while still maintaining quality.

Tracks Brushing

Must detect if brushing actually happens, not just time it.

Parent Notifications

Parents should know if brushing was completed or skipped.

2-Minute Music Timer

Keeps kids engaged for the full brushing time.

Comfort + Size

Must fit a child’s mouth and be comfortable to use.

Long-Term Use

Needs to last over time and allow replacement heads.

Competitors / Existing Solutions

Colgate hum Kids

Fun app + tracking.

Issue: Expensive and hard to maintain long-term.

Philips Sonicare Kids

2-minute timer with app.

Issue: Kids can fake brushing, weak parent tracking.

7AM2M Electric Toothbrush

30-second reminders and long battery life.

Issue: Not engaging and lacks monitoring.

Potential Solutions

Music Timer Toothbrush

Fun, but doesn’t verify brushing.

App-Based Tracker

Easy to build, but relies on honesty.

Parent Monitoring Device

Strong accountability, but not engaging.

Brush Buddy (Final Idea)

Combines fun, tracking, and parent notifications.

Chosen Design

We chose Brush Buddy because it combines entertainment, tracking, and accountability. Unlike other solutions, it doesn’t just time brushing — it helps confirm that brushing actually happens.

Competitive Advantage (why design is innovative)

Brush Buddy stands out because it does more than just encourage brushing — it verifies it. Most competitors rely on timers or apps, but our design adds tracking and parent notifications, making it more reliable and useful.

Design Changes Made Based on Feedback

Our design remained consistent throughout the ideation process. Feedback confirmed that our original idea was already the strongest because it combined fun, tracking, and accountability in one system.
